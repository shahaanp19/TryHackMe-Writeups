# Cybersecurity Fubdamentals
---
## Objective
The purpose of these rooms was to build a foundational understanding of cybersecurity, including the different domains within the field, the roles involved, and the core principles of both offensive and defensive security.

## Overview of Cybersecurity
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, exploitation, or damage.
It involves multiple disciplines, including network security, application security, and incident response. Understanding these areas is essential for identifying vulnerabilities and defending against attacks.

---

## Offensive Security Skills
These rooms introduced the core mindset and techniques used in ethical hacking.

Skills developed include:
- Understanding the stages of an attack (reconnaissance, enumeration, exploitation)
- Thinking from an attacker’s perspective when assessing systems
- Identifying potential weaknesses in applications and networks

These skills are directly applicable to ethical hacking, as penetration testers must simulate real-world attacks to uncover vulnerabilities before they are exploited.

---

## Defensive Security Awareness
Understanding defensive security provides insight into how attacks are detected and mitigated.

Skills developed include:
- Recognizing indicators of compromise
- Understanding how monitoring and alerting systems work
- Learning how defenders analyze suspicious activity

For ethical hackers, this knowledge is important because it highlights how actions may be detected during testing and how security controls can be improved.

---

## SIEM and Monitoring Skills
The introduction to SIEM systems provided exposure to how security events are collected and analyzed.

Skills developed include:
- Interpreting logs and security events
- Understanding how multiple data sources are correlated
- Identifying unusual or malicious behavior

In ethical hacking, this helps in understanding how attacks are tracked and investigated, which is critical when evaluating the effectiveness of defensive systems.

---

## Understanding Security Roles
These rooms provided clarity on how different cybersecurity roles interact within an organization.

Skills developed include:
- Understanding the responsibilities of offensive and defensive teams
- Recognizing how security testing fits into broader security operations
- Identifying how vulnerabilities are reported and addressed

This is important in ethical hacking, as penetration testers must communicate findings clearly and work within structured security processes.

---

## Foundational Cybersecurity Skills
These rooms reinforced essential concepts that support ethical hacking.

Skills developed include:
- Understanding basic security principles
- Recognizing common attack surfaces
- Building a structured approach to learning cybersecurity

These foundational skills are necessary before progressing to more technical areas such as enumeration, exploitation, and privilege escalation.

---

## Practical Application to Ethical Hacking
The combined knowledge from these rooms supports the early stages of ethical hacking by enabling:
- A structured approach to attacking systems
- Awareness of how defenses operate
- The ability to think critically about vulnerabilities

This foundation is essential for progressing into hands-on technical skills such as network scanning, web exploitation, and system enumeration.

---

## Conclusion
These rooms contributed to building the core mindset and foundational skills required for ethical hacking. They provide the necessary understanding of both attack methodologies and defensive mechanisms, which is critical for developing practical cybersecurity expertise.
